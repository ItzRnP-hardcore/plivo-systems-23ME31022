Architecture Summary: The Flaky Network

1. Design Philosophy

The system streams a 160-byte frame every 20ms across a relay that drops, delays, reorders, and duplicates packets, under a ≤ 1% deadline-miss cap and a ≤ 2.0x bandwidth cap, minimizing playout delay. The design is a hybrid: proactive dual-coverage XOR FEC as the primary defense (zero added round trips), with NACK-triggered retransmission over the otherwise idle feedback lane as a surgical backstop for the burst losses FEC cannot cover.

Pure ARQ was rejected as the primary mechanism: a retransmission needs gap detection plus two crossings of the hostile network (each 20–80ms), which forces the delay score far higher than FEC does. Pure FEC via plain replication was implemented first and rejected after measurement: a single repair copy travels close to its original, so burst loss kills both together (measured 2–5% miss on a Gilbert-Elliott burst profile where the final design achieves 0.4–0.6%).

where X_i = P(i-2) XOR P(i-K). Every frame j is therefore covered by two independent repair packets: X_(j+2) (fast repair, 40ms later; its partner j+2-K is old and almost surely already delivered) and X_(j+K) (burst-safe repair; its partner j+K-2 lies beyond any burst of length ≤ K-2). Two recovery chances cost exactly the 160 bytes plain replication spends on one — XOR strictly dominates replication here.

3. Adaptive Burst Spacing

Surviving a burst of L consecutive drops requires repair data spaced ≥ L frames from the original, which in turn requires the playout delay to cover L×20ms + max_jitter. This bound is physics, not implementation. Rather than hardcoding the trade-off, both binaries derive K = clamp((DELAY_MS - 80)/20, 1, 8) from the environment at startup: whatever delay the run is graded at, the protocol automatically uses the deepest burst spacing that delay affords. At K ≤ 2 the two XOR lags coincide, so the sender degrades gracefully to plain replication of P(i-K).

4. Bandwidth Budgeting

The relay counts every byte it ingests on both lanes, including packets it then drops, against frames × 160. Since the source→sender leg is local and lossless, the sender knows its exact overhead deterministically. FEC attachment is gated at a running 1.975x and retransmits at 1.99x, so the 2.0x cap holds even when retransmits cluster at the end of a run (an earlier version violated the cap exactly this way). Measured overhead: 1.97–1.98x; feedback traffic ~90B per 30s run.
