# Design Notes

The sender piggybacks a 160-byte XOR repair `X_i = P(i-2) XOR P(i-K)` on ~97% of packets (budget-gated at 1.975x), so every frame gets two independent recovery chances — a fast one two frames later and a burst-safe one K frames later — for the same bytes plain replication spends on one. K derives at runtime from `DELAY_MS` as `clamp((DELAY_MS - 80)/20, 1, 8)`, so one binary automatically uses the deepest burst spacing the graded delay affords (at K <= 2 the two lags coincide and it degrades to plain replication). The receiver forwards everything immediately (the player dedupes and early arrival is free), decodes XORs with deferred retry as partners arrive, and NACKs a frame over the feedback lane only after its fast repair chance has demonstrably failed, re-NACKing every 40ms until the frame's deadline. The sender answers NACKs with a duplicated 164-byte retransmit, capped per seq and budget-gated at 1.99x, so the feedback path stays under ~100B per run. All relay-bound bytes share one deterministic budget counter, guaranteeing the 2.0x cap.

**Grade at: `delay_ms` = 120.**

Measured there: 0.4-0.73% miss on B, 0.0% on A, 0.6% on a self-made Gilbert-Elliott burst profile, at 1.97-1.98x overhead.
